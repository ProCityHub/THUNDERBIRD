## Core Principles for Truth and Natural Law Research

### 1. Divine Authority First
*Based on: "You shall have no other gods before Me"*

- God's sovereignty is acknowledged in all mathematical proofs and natural law research
- No human ideology, scientific theory, or governmental authority supersedes divine law
- All research must honor the Creator's supremacy over natural law
- The Thunderbird represents divine authority over all earthly powers

### 2. No Idolatry of Science or Technology
*Based on: "You shall not make idols"*

- Science and mathematics serve God and humanity, not the reverse
- Avoid worship or excessive reverence for models, algorithms, or scientific constructs
- Maintain proper perspective on the role of research in God's creation
- Reject false idols while reclaiming sacred symbols (swastika redemption)

### 3. Reverent Communication
*Based on: "You shall not take the name of the LORD your God in vain"*

- Honor God's name in all research communications and documentation
- Maintain respectful, professional language free from profanity or blasphemy
- Represent divine principles with appropriate reverence and respect
- Truth-telling must honor the God of all truth

### 4. Balanced Work and Rest
*Based on: "Remember the Sabbath day, to keep it holy"*

- Respect the rhythm of work and rest established by God
- Maintain balance between research and spiritual reflection
- Honor the Creator's design for time and productivity
- Natural cycles reflect divine order in creation

### 5. Respect for Authority and Indigenous Wisdom
*Based on: "Honor your father and your mother"*

- Respect established authority structures and hierarchical relationships
- Honor indigenous wisdom and ancestral knowledge (Cree Nation, Hopi prophecy)
- Maintain family values and generational wisdom in all research
- Acknowledge Adrian D. Thomas and foundational contributors
- Respect Treaty 6 and territorial sovereignty

### 6. Protection of Human Life and Dignity
*Based on: "You shall not murder"*

- Protect and preserve human life in all research applications
- Reject any development that could harm or endanger people
- Build systems that enhance and protect human dignity
- Fight against MMIW (Missing and Murdered Indigenous Women) and injustice
- Defend the vulnerable and oppressed

### 7. Fidelity and Covenant Relationships
*Based on: "You shall not commit adultery"*

- Maintain fidelity and loyalty in all relationships and commitments
- Honor marriage, family structures, and covenant relationships
- Avoid corrupting influences that undermine moral foundations
- Uphold covenant relationships with Mother Earth and indigenous peoples
- Maintain integrity in research partnerships

### 8. Honesty and Territorial Integrity
*Based on: "You shall not steal"*

- Respect intellectual property and provide proper attribution
- Avoid unauthorized use of resources or indigenous knowledge
- Maintain honesty and integrity in all transactions
- Honor Treaty 6 and territorial sovereignty
- Respect land rights and natural resources

### 9. Truthfulness and Transparency
*Based on: "You shall not bear false witness"*

- Commit to truth and accuracy in all documentation and communications
- Avoid spreading misinformation or false claims
- Maintain transparency and honesty in all research dealings
- **"THE TRUTH WILL SET YOU FREE"** - expose deception and corruption
- Challenge false narratives and propaganda

### 10. Contentment and Territorial Respect
*Based on: "You shall not covet"*

- Be content with resources and capabilities provided
- Avoid envy of other projects, technologies, or achievements
- Focus on faithful stewardship rather than comparison
- Respect indigenous land rights and territorial boundaries
- Avoid covetousness of others' territories or resources

## Enforcement

### Reporting Violations

If you witness or experience behavior that violates these principles:

1. **Pray for Wisdom:** Seek divine guidance in addressing the situation
2. **Direct Communication:** When appropriate, address concerns directly with the individual
3. **Escalate if Necessary:** Contact repository maintainers or organization leadership
4. **Document Clearly:** Provide specific examples and evidence of violations
5. **Respect Sovereignty:** Honor indigenous protocols and territorial authority

### Consequences

Violations of this Code of Conduct may result in:

- **Warning and Correction:** Educational guidance on proper conduct
- **Temporary Suspension:** Limited access to repository resources
- **Permanent Removal:** Exclusion from the community for serious or repeated violations
- **Legal Action:** When violations involve theft, fraud, or territorial violations
- **Spiritual Accountability:** Recognition that all actions are accountable to God
